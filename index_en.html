Marine Robotics Workshop

March 3 to 7, 2025, Buenos Aires, Argentina

We announce the first national event related to marine robotics in Argentina to be held in March 2025 in Buenos Aires!

Thanks to an allowance received from the Williams Foundation , we will be able to welcome Eng. Massimo Caccia from the CNR of Italy, an international expert in marine robotics. The event consists of two activities: a course (March 3-5, 2025) and a workshop (March 6-7, 2025).

Motivation

  • What is marine robotics used for?

    It is used to perform operations in inaccessible and/or hazardous locations (such as beneath a glacier or in deep-sea environments); to carry out repetitive tasks (for example, water monitoring or bathymetric surveys); and to cooperate with humans (robots can assist or support divers).

Objectives

Course of Marine Robotics:

state of the art and its applications

  • We invite the academic community, researchers, doctoral and master students, advanced students from technical schools, industry, SMEs and members of public institutions to participate in the course Marine Robotics: state of the art and its applications to be held on March 3, 4 and 5, 2025 at the Escuela de Ciencias del Mar, Av. Antártida Argentina 425, Ciudad Autónoma de Buenos Aires.

  • The course aims to provide basic knowledge about marine robotics and its different applications to an audience of researchers and students, as well as to generate a multi and interdisciplinary interest in marine robotics in current professionals and new generations.

  • Classes will be held in English language and divided into modules:
    • M1. Marine robots and their applications.
    • M2. Modelling and identification.
    • M3. Cooperative control of unmanned marine vehicle formation.
    • M4. Navigation: sensors and algorithms.
    • M5. New trends in marine robotics: research and technological challenges.
    • M6. Marine robotics applications in polar and extreme areas.
  • Modality: The course will have a limited hybrid modality, so it is requested that residents of the AMBA and the city of La Plata choose the face-to-face modality.

  • Registration deadline: December 20, 2024.

  • The course requires registration and participation will be confirmed prior to the event.

  • For further information, please contact robotica.marina.2025@gmail.com

Teacher




Eng. Massimo Caccia

Born in Genoa (Italy) in 1966, he graduated in Electronic Engineering from the University of Genoa in January 1991. He immediately obtained a contract with the Telerobot consortium (Finmeccanica group) to develop embedded real-time control architectures for advanced robotic systems (vehicles and arms) and then a fellowship at Ansaldo DNU-RTL to address the topic of acoustic navigation, guidance and control of ground vehicles in structured environments.

In 1993, he joined Centro Nazionale delle Ricerche (CNR) to deepen his research activities, theoretical and applied, in the field of marine robotics. From 2013 to 2018, he was Director of the Istituto degli Studi sui Sistemi Intelligenti per l'Automazione (ISSIA-CNR) at the Genoa and Palermo sites. He is currently Director of Research at the Institute of Naval Engineering (INM-CNR, Genoa).

His scientific interests are focused on navigation and cooperative control, navigation and guidance of heterogeneous unmanned marine vehicles; modeling and identification of unmanned marine vehicles; design and development of marine robot prototypes; and marine robotics in polar waters. At the European level, he pioneered the application of robotics for maritime safety, the use of the first autonomous robotic vessel in Antarctica and of the first robotic vessel to sample water below an Arctic glacier.

He developed a methodology to characterize the hydrodynamic model of underwater and surface robots using simple on-board sensors and he and his group are currently working to develop the first guidance and control system for marine robotic vehicles based on learning-by-imitation of humans. His research is supported by more than 200 publications in books, journals and conference papers.

He led different projects, both national and international, among which stand out: MONUSEN, Horizon Europe Twinning, a project focused on driving the University of Montenegro (Faculty of Electrical Engineering) towards a path of excellence in the field of underwater sensor networks (USN); Blue RoSES - Blue Robotics for Sustainable Eco-friendly Services, EASME EMFF-BlueEconomy, whose objectives are:

  • 1) To bring citizens closer to the possibility of visiting the submarine world through the adapted use, integrating robotics and IoT, of marinas and recreational boats.
  • 2) To monitor with ROVs the quality of water and seabed, as well as to verify the condition of yacht hulls.

He is a member of the Board of Directors of the Maritime Technology Cluster MareFVG. He was Chair of the 9th IFAC Conference on Manoeuvring and Control of Marine Craft 2012, and Exhibit Chair of Oceans'15 MTS/IEEE Conference 2015; Coordinator of the Topic Group Marine Robotics of euRobotics aisbl (2014-2016); member of the Board of Directors of the Distretto Ligure delle Tecnologie Marine (2011-2017); and member of the Scientific Technical Committee on innovation in shipyards of the Italian Ministry of Infrastructures and Transport (2010-2012).
